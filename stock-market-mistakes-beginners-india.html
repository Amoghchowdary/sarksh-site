Beginner mistakes

Most beginner mistakes are not technical. They are behavioural.

Learn common stock market mistakes beginners make in India: chasing tips, ignoring risk, using borrowed money, not understanding charges, and confusing trading with investing.

Chasing tips before learning basics

The first mistake is entering the market through tips, reels, groups or profit stories before understanding demat accounts, order types, charges, taxes, risk and exits.

Using money that should not be risked

Borrowed money and essential household or business money should not be treated as market capital. A beginner must separate survival money from risk capital.

Ignoring charges and statements

Brokerage, taxes, DP charges, platform charges and other costs can affect real outcomes. A serious beginner checks statements and understands costs instead of only watching price movement.

No written process

Without a written process, decisions become emotional. Write the reason for entry, expected holding period, risk boundary and exit plan. It does not guarantee success, but it builds discipline.

Common questions

Can beginners avoid all mistakes?

No. Mistakes happen. The aim is to reduce avoidable damage and learn through structure.

Should a beginner start from a webinar?

A structured education session can help a beginner understand the basics before making product or platform decisions.
